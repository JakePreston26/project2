Scientific Data

Prison Population
There are over 2.3 million people incarcerated in the United States. That is 716 people to to every 100,000 people getting incarcerated. The U.S. makes up about 4.4% of the world's population, but it has about 22% of the world's prisoners in its prisons. There are over 540,000 people in prison who have not stood trial yet, that is almost a quarter of the entire U.S. prison population in America. the number of convicted people has remained steady, in recent years on the decline, while the number of people in pretrial prison has been growing every year.
Crime Statistics
There are over 1 million drug arrests every year, the majority of which are for possession rather that sales/manufactoring of drugs. New York's "war on drugs" has largely declined, dropping from around 35% in the 1990s, to around 11-12% in 2015. 51% of people convicted of a drug crime are convicted of a similar offense within 3 years, while public order and property convictions follow closely behind at 50% and 45% respectively. The Majority of youths in prison are held for nonviolent crimes, or no crimes at all. there are over 5,000 children in the Adult Justice system. There are 85,000 people confined because of immigration.
